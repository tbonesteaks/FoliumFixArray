Denver Police Data Visualized Using Python, Folium, & HeatMap

This data is politically charged, so I will not be adding an explanation with these maps and visualizations. This data is capable of speaking for itself, and viewers are encouraged to draw their own conclusions. This is not a Rorschach test. This is to show Folium's capability of illustrating, via Heat Map, half a million coordinates on a map under 15mb. The automation and backend programming are shared under the MIT license. Top links display whole dataset maps (500,000+ Plot Points). Links below are separated by year, include access to the raw dataset source, and a tutorial.

Some Numbers:

523,149 Registered Voters reside in Denver; 260,422 Males, 262,727 Females.
The DPD reports 46,047 DV incidents between 1/1/2016 and 10/2/2021. ~9,000/yr.
If each incident was a different registered male voter less than 17% of those men are involved.
(but they're not felons)

If we look at the population of Denver, 715,522, 48% being men, there are 343,500 men. If each incident is a different person, 13.4% of men are involved.
(but doesn't account for recidivism)

If we assume each perpetrator acted at least twice, 6.7% of men are involved. If we assume each perpetrator acted at least thrice, 4.4% of men are involved.
But this doesn't account for transients, vacationing, and men traveling for business.

Or that some of these perpetrators were women.

The author does not condone, excuse, or encourage domestic violence in any form.
